Watermelons: Past, Present, and Future

The Past

Watermelon, a vine plant, originally came from West Africa. The plant is know for its distinct pinkish-red fleshy center, that is sweet in taste. Colonists and slaves coming from Africa introduced watermelon to the rest of the world.

The Present

Currently, China is the worlds largest watermelon producer. There are more than 1200 kinds of watermelons today. The most common one, a seedless variety found in grocery stores, is the product of cross-breeding.

The Future

The future of watermelons will be defined by scientific advancement and public opinion. Public perception of genetically modified organisms is the biggest hurdle facing the future advancement of watermelons. As the world gets hotter due to climate change, and hungrier due to overpopulation, the demand for watermelon will increase exponentially. Supply will only meet demand if we as a society decide to accept GMO's. Without this, we could be met with a world where a single watermelon costs $50 or more.
